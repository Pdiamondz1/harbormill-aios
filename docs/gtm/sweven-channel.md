# Sweven Channel Playbook

Sweven (the partner's coworking/membership workspace for entrepreneurs) is Harbormill's unfair
advantage on the **hardest part of the funnel: getting in front of warm business owners.** It
replaces cold list-building at the top of the existing [30-Day Playbook](README.md) — it does **not**
replace the ladder. Sweven feeds intros; the [intro call](intro-call-script.md) → **Loop Audit** →
**Focused Project** → **Retainer** ladder runs exactly as written.

> Sweven is a **ready-made Warm-50+**: a room already full of A-tier contacts (owners *and* buyers).
> The whole point is that the top of the funnel arrives pre-warmed.

---

## 1. Sweven as the Warm-50 engine

- Run the [Sweven membership audit](sweven-audit.md) → every `open_to_pilot = Y` member becomes an
  **A-tier** row in the [Warm-50 tracker](warm-50-tracker.md).
- The audit also names the **beachhead agency sub-type** ([beachhead-decision.md](beachhead-decision.md)) —
  concentrate intros there first so the loops you build are reusable.
- Because these contacts are warm + local + already trust the space, the cold-outreach friction in
  [outreach-templates.md](outreach-templates.md) mostly disappears; outreach becomes "grab a coffee
  here" instead of a DM.

## 2. The demo night (intro-generation event)

A monthly "kill the busywork" session at Sweven. **One job: book intro calls before people leave.**

### Run-of-show (~25–30 min + mingle)
1. **Hook — the operator trap (3 min).** "Most owners spend their week as the highest-paid admin in
   the building. Show of hands: who re-keys the same data / chases the same invoices / builds the same
   report every week?"
2. **The shift (4 min).** A retainer for a service business — but AI agents deliver the service. The
   moat is a system with *persistent context that compounds*, not a chatbot. Briefly: the AIOS deck +
   "loops."
3. **Live proof (10 min).** Walk the **two flagship case studies** ([restaurant + Sweven](case-studies/))
   on the actual deck — the branded command center + the Value-Delivered ROI surface. Real numbers,
   their own space (Sweven) as one of the examples = instant credibility.
4. **The method (4 min).** The **Four-Condition Loop Test** on one example (teaches + positions you as
   the guide, not a black-box vendor). "This is how we pick what's worth automating first."
5. **The offer + CTA (4 min).** "I'm doing a handful of free 30-min sessions — I map your repeating
   work and point at the one loop worth building first; you keep the plan either way. Sign up on the
   sheet by the door / scan this — let's book it tonight."
6. **Mingle + book live.** Calendar open; book intros on the spot. Every "not now" → the referral ask.

### After the night
- Log every booked intro in [warm-50-tracker.md](warm-50-tracker.md) (status → `Intro booked`).
- Send the [warm-intro / follow-up templates](outreach-templates.md) same day, before threads cool.
- From here it's the **standard ladder** — nothing bespoke.

## 4. Guardrails

- **Don't pitch from stage.** The demo night sells the *intro call*, not a build. Guide, don't sell.
- **Concentrate.** Lead intros with the beachhead agency sub-type; route restaurants/other service
  businesses to the dogfood/case-study track, solo founders to the Phase-2 parking lot.
- **Protect the relationship.** It's the partner's space and members — keep it useful and low-key;
  a bad-feeling "sales event" burns the channel. Value first, every time.
