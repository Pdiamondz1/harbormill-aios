## 1. The pitch ladder

Three lengths of the same pitch, each tuned for an **agency owner**. Every one follows the same
spine:

1. **The repeating pain** they feel every week (re-keying leads, chasing AR, rebuilding client reports).
2. **The dollar prize** — name that the pain is money, and that it recurs.
3. **The payoff line:** *AI agents run the repeating work, and you watch the ROI on one screen.*

Pick the length to match how warm the moment is. Don't pitch a build — pitch the *idea*, then land
the free intro / Loop Audit.

### 30-second — the elevator ("so what do you do?")

> *"You know how an agency runs on the same busywork every week — re-keying leads into the CRM,
> chasing clients for overdue invoices, rebuilding the same status report for every account? That's
> real money. It's hours your team bills out, and it's cash sitting in late invoices.*
>
> *I build AI agents that run that work for you — on a schedule, the same way every time. And it all
> lands on one deck where you watch the ROI: exactly what was recovered, against what you pay.*
>
> *It's not a chatbot. It's automation you can leave running. Worth a 30-minute look?"*

### 2-minute — the warm intro (someone's interested, give them the shape)

> *"So here's the world I work in. Agencies are profitable on the work — and they leak time and cash
> on the work* around *the work. Every week, someone's re-keying new leads into the CRM. Someone's
> chasing the same overdue invoices. Someone's rebuilding the same client report for the tenth time.*
>
> *Put a number on it. Say it's twelve minutes a lead, eight leads a day — that's ten work-weeks a
> year, just re-typing. Or you've got forty-some-thousand in invoices sitting past thirty days,
> because follow-up is whoever remembers. That's not a software problem. That's money on the floor.*
>
> *What I do is build that repeating work as an AI agent — what I call a Loop. It runs on a schedule,
> it checks its own work against a rule before it acts, and anything that touches a client waits for
> your one-click approval. It works while you sleep.*
>
> *And here's the part that makes it real: it all runs on one operating deck — your live numbers up
> top, and a page whose only job is the scoreboard. It shows exactly what the automation recovered
> this month, against the fee. No black box. You watch the ROI on one screen.*
>
> *The way in is a free 30-minute call. I map your repeating work and point at the one Loop worth
> building first — and you keep that plan either way."*

### 5-minute — the engaged prospect (add the method + the deck)

> *"Let me give you the whole picture, because this is more than 'we'll automate something.'*
>
> ***The problem first.*** *Agencies don't lose to the work — you're great at the work. You lose to
> the busywork around it. Every week it's the same three things: leads getting re-keyed by hand from
> forms and inboxes into the CRM; invoices going uncollected because chasing them is manual and
> awkward; client reports rebuilt from scratch every single time. It repeats, it's expensive, and
> it's invisible because it's spread across your team.*
>
> ***Make it a number.*** *Take leads. Twelve minutes each, eight a day — that's four hundred hours a
> year. Ten work-weeks of someone re-typing what a machine could route in seconds. Or take AR: forty
> grand stuck past thirty days. Consistent follow-up typically pulls forward about a third of that —
> fifteen thousand back in your account — and it keeps doing it, every cycle. That's the prize.*
>
> ***Now the method, because this is the part nobody else does.*** *I don't just automate the loudest
> thing. I run every repeating task through four questions. One — does it repeat on a schedule? Two —
> is there a clear rule that decides when it's done right, not someone's gut? Three — if a run goes
> wrong, is that cheap to undo? Four — can the AI actually reach the data and take the action? The
> work that passes all four, I rank by value-per-effort, and we build the highest one first. That's
> the Loop Audit. It's my signature method, and it's why you don't waste the first build.*
>
> ***Then the deck.*** *Everything runs on your own operating deck — your Supabase, your Google Cloud,
> your AI keys. Your data, your account, never mixed with anyone else's. Up top: your live KPIs, the
> ones an agency owner actually watches. Every week, an AI-written brief in plain English — what moved,
> what to watch. A Loop running the AR follow-up or the lead intake, with anything client-facing
> waiting for your approval. An assistant, Aria, that answers from* your *real numbers, not the open
> internet. And the one I care about most — a Value page whose only job is the honest scoreboard:
> dollars recovered this month, against the fee. So you're never wondering if it's working. You can
> see it.*
>
> ***Why it compounds.*** *A Zapier chain or a one-off script has no memory. This builds a context
> layer — your metrics history, your briefs, your findings, your playbooks — and the longer it runs,
> the more it knows your business. It gets harder to live without, not easier.*
>
> ***The way in.*** *A free 30-minute call. I map your repeating work, score it on those four
> conditions, and point at the one Loop worth building first. If we go further, that's the Loop Audit
> — five hundred to twenty-five hundred, fixed scope, and I credit the whole fee toward the build.
> You keep the ranked plan either way. Want to grab a time?"*

> **The one sentence under all three:** *AI agents run your repeating work — and you watch the ROI on
> one screen.* If you forget everything else, land that.

## 2. Positioning vs. the alternatives

When an agency owner hears "AI automation," they reach for the four things they already know. Here's
the honest read on each — and the one line that separates you. This table is the spoken-pitch mirror
of the [Part 3 objection-handling playbook](03-run-the-call.md#3-objection-handling-playbook); say it
the same way there.

| They compare you to… | What it actually is | Why Harbormill is different |
|---|---|---|
| **A VA / offshore hire** | A recurring monthly cost that forgets everything when they leave and caps out at human speed. | A one-time build you *own* — runs 24/7, never quits, and the context compounds instead of walking out the door. |
| **Zapier / Make** | A fixed if-this-then-that chain. No memory, no judgment, and it can't tell you when it quietly broke. | A Loop carries persistent context *and checks its own work against a rule* before it acts — and flags what it's unsure of. A teammate, not a tripwire. |
| **A junior dev scripting it** | A one-off script that rots — no one maintains it, no context layer, a liability you can't read. | A mature, battle-tested deck that gets *your* logo and *your* data — and I keep it healthy. A maintained asset, not a babysitting job. |
| **ChatGPT** | A generic chatbot that starts from zero every conversation and can't actually *do* anything. | Grounded in *your* live numbers and playbooks, gets smarter on your business the longer it runs, and the Loops do the task on a schedule — not just talk about it. |

> **The differentiator line — say it out loud:**
> *"All of those are one-off scripts or generic chatbots. What I build is the opposite: persistent,
> compounding context plus self-checking Loops you can leave running. It remembers your business, it
> checks its own work, and it gets more valuable the longer it runs."*

That single sentence — **persistent compounding context + self-checking Loops you can leave running**
— closes every one of the four comparisons. Pair it with the prize number from the
[worksheet](03-run-the-call.md#2-make-the-prize-a-number-worksheet) and the comparison turns into a
math problem in your favor.

## 3. The demo run-of-show (live deck)

Six beats. Each is a **"Say this" / "Show this"** split — *Say* is spoken, *Show* names the exact
deck surface from the [Part 1 surface tour](01-know-the-product.md#2-surface-by-surface-tour). This is
the [demo-night walk](../sweven-channel.md#2-the-demo-night-intro-generation-event) compressed to one
prospect: same thesis (a retainer where AI agents deliver the service; the moat is compounding
context, not a chatbot), same flow (command center → live proof → method → offer).

Keep it under ten minutes. The demo's job is not to impress — it's to make the free intro / Loop Audit
feel obvious. **On-screen numbers are illustrative;** say so once, up front: *"This is a sample deck —
the numbers are illustrative, but the surfaces are exactly what yours would be."*

### Beat 1 — The boring-on-purpose open

- **Say this:** *"This is your whole business on one screen. That's the point — nothing flashy. Your
  live numbers, and one card that tells you what this engagement has paid back. Most owners are flying
  between five tabs to get this. Here it's one glance."*
- **Show this:** **Overview** — the post-login home: live KPI cards plus the "value delivered" hero card.

### Beat 2 — The command center

- **Say this:** *"These are the numbers an agency owner actually watches — recurring revenue,
  utilization, pipeline, churn. Live, color-coded: green is fine, amber's slipping, red needs you.
  And once a week, this turns into a plain-English brief — what moved, what to watch — written for you,
  not a wall of charts."*
- **Show this:** **Overview** KPI cards (the live command center) → click into **Briefings** for the
  weekly AI-written brief.

### Beat 3 — A Loop running ("this works while you sleep")

- **Say this:** *"Here's where it stops being a dashboard. This is a Loop — an AI agent running the AR
  follow-up [or lead intake] on a schedule. It chased every overdue invoice this week, the same way
  every time. Anything that touches a client waits right here for your one-click approval. This ran
  while you slept."*
- **Show this:** **Loops** *(admin-only)* — the configured Loop and its human-approval queue → and the
  **Finding** it filed when a KPI breached (the system catching the problem itself).

### Beat 4 — The Value-Delivered ROI moment

- **Say this:** *"This is the page I care about most. Its only job is the honest scoreboard — dollars
  recovered, hours saved, cost avoided this month, totaled against the fee. So if this deck recovered
  [illustrative] $18k this month against a $3,000 retainer, that number is right here. You never have
  to wonder if it's working. You can see it."*
- **Show this:** **Value** — the ROI page: value delivered rolled up versus the fee, with the ROI
  multiple, reconciled against what the audit promised.

### Beat 5 — Aria, grounded in those numbers

- **Say this:** *"And you can just ask. 'How are we doing this week?' 'What's driving churn?' 'What
  should I do first today?' It answers from* your *real numbers and your own playbooks — not the open
  internet, not a guess. It's grounded, and it can only pull the safe levers I gave it."*
- **Show this:** **Assistant (Aria)** — ask one agency question and let her answer from the live
  metrics and knowledge base.

### Beat 6 — The "this runs itself / here's what we'd build first for YOU" close

- **Say this:** *"So that's it — it watches your numbers, writes your brief, runs the repeating work,
  and shows you the ROI, mostly while you're doing literally anything else. The honest question isn't
  'is this cool.' It's 'which Loop is worth building first for* you*.' That's exactly what the Loop
  Audit answers — I map your repeating work, score it, and hand you the ranked plan. Five hundred to
  twenty-five hundred, fixed scope, and I credit the whole fee toward the build. Want to grab a time?"*
- **Show this:** Back to **Overview** (full picture) → close on the **Loop Audit** offer (the
  prospecting **Audits** surface is *your* instrument behind it — don't show it; mention the
  deliverable: a ranked Opportunity Report within 48 hours).

> Hand off to the close in [Part 3 §4](03-run-the-call.md#4-the-close) and the
> [intro-call script's close](../intro-call-script.md#5-book-it-before-you-hang-up). **Book the Loop
> Audit before you hang up.**

later build. So keep a screenshot deck that walks the **exact same 6 beats**, in the same order, with
the same spoken lines from §3. You narrate live; the screenshots just stand in for the click-through.

**Source the screenshots, clearly labeled "illustrative," from what already exists today:**

- **Primary source — the website demo tour** (`website/src/components/demo/`, live on harbormill.net):
  it's already agency-toned (MRR, churn, NPS, pipeline) and already labeled *"Illustrative sample data
  — not a live client account."* It covers **Overview**, **Weekly briefing**, and **Ask Aria** out of
  the box — three of the six beats.
- **Secondary source — the `demo/restaurant-ops` deck / the restaurant case study** for any surface the
  website tour doesn't stage (Loops/finding, Value). **Note:** that deck isn't in the repo yet; until
  it exists, screenshot the corresponding surface from the seeded product deck (`supabase/seed.sql`
  data) or stage it from Part 1's surface descriptions. **Whatever the source, watermark every slide
  "Illustrative — sample data."**
