# Say This, Not That — the no-jargon translation card

> **Audience: you, Damon — before and during any prospect or client conversation.**
> One rule: **sell the hole, not the drill.** A prospect doesn't want an "AI operating deck" — they
> want their Saturdays back and their invoices paid. Lead with *their* outcome; the product names are
> just what you call it *after* they care.

This is a reference card in the [Field Guide](00-index.md). It's the discipline behind the
prospect-facing language in [Part 2 — Pitch & Demo](02-pitch-and-demo.md) and the
[one-pager](one-pager.md).

## The rule of thumb

- **Internal docs (Parts 1–4) keep the precise words** — *you* need "Loop," "AIOS," "report-ingest"
  to think clearly. **Prospects never hear them cold.**
- A tech noun is allowed **only after** the outcome has landed, and only as *"we call that a ___."*
  ("AIOS / operating deck" doesn't even survive that — it names the *thing*, not the *result*.)
- If a sentence explains **how it works** before **what they get**, flip it.
- Their pain, their words, their number — first. Always.

## The translation

| Don't say (tech) | Say (their world) |
|---|---|
| Harbormill AIOS / an AI operating deck | "one screen that shows what's working, what's slipping, and what needs you today" |
| I'll build you a Loop | "I'll set up the invoice-chasing so it just happens — correctly, without you" |
| a Loop Audit | "a quick workup to find where you're losing the most time and money, and the one fix worth doing first" |
| the Four-Condition Loop Test | *(never say it)* → "I only automate things that are safe to run on their own and pay for themselves" |
| the Value-Delivered / ROI surface | "a running tally of the hours and dollars this has saved you" |
| Aria, grounded in your metrics | "ask it anything about your business in plain English — it answers from your real numbers" |
| KPIs / metrics / dashboard | "your numbers, at a glance" |
| report-ingest / the data seam / RAG / embeddings | *(never — these stay in the engine room)* |
| white-label / per-client deploy | "it runs on your own accounts and keys — your data, never mixed with anyone else's" |
| connectors / pg_cron / edge functions | "it plugs into the tools you already use" |

## Three openers that lead with them, not the tech

- *"What's the thing you do every week that you most wish would just handle itself?"*
- *"Where do you feel like you're leaving money on the floor — late invoices, dropped leads, something else?"*
- *"If you got one afternoon a week back, what would you actually do with it?"*

> **The tell:** if a prospect's eyes glaze or they ask "so what is it, exactly?", you led with the
> drill. Back up to the hole — the hours, the cash, the headache they'd hand off — and the *what-is-it*
> answers itself.
