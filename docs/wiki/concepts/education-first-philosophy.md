# Education-First Philosophy

Harbormill Automation's core differentiator and north star: **teach the owner to
use AI (with Claude) first, then automate** — "teach you to fish," not sell a
black box ([[Project Context]]). It's the lead pillar on the [[Marketing Site]]
and shapes Rung 0 of [[The Harbormill Ladder]] (a hands-on setup session).

## Why it holds up (external evidence)

The market data says capability — not tooling — is the bottleneck, which is
exactly what education-first attacks:

- **Most AI programs fail on the people side:** ~70% of AI/digital transformations
  fall short because employees can't integrate AI into real decisions, not because
  of the tech ([Grant Thornton](https://www.grantthornton.com/insights/articles/advisory/2026/ai-adoption-strategies-that-stick)).
- **Training has hard ROI:** formal AI training returns ~**$3.70 per $1**, and
  trained employees are ~**2.7× more proficient** than self-taught
  ([Vinsys](https://www.vinsys.com/blog/ai-training-for-businesses-from-adoption-to-real-roi-in-2026),
  [Iternal](https://iternal.ai/ai-training-for-employees)).
- **For SMBs, hands-on beats abstract:** training should be applied to day-to-day
  work in the tools the team already uses
  ([Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/blog/small-business/ai-literacy-for-small-business/)).

## How it shows up in the build

The whole point of [[Harbormill AIOS]] is an artifact the owner actually reads and
operates (metrics + brief + [[Aria]]) — capability embedded in the product, not
locked behind the consultant. Contrast with the tool-heavy field in
[[SMB AI-Automation Landscape]].
